+ bio

I got my start in journalism as a dual enrollment student at Guilford College in Greensboro, N.C. My senior year, I was faced with the choice of either Victorian literature or journalism to fulfill an English requirement. I chose the student newspaper, where I investigated spurious retention bonuses and revealed the college built a coffeeshop without a building permit. I've been hooked since then.

In May 2018, I graduated from Virginia Tech summa cum laude with a bachelor’s degree in mathematics. While I was at Tech, I served in a number of roles for the student newspaper, The Collegiate Times, ranging from local government beat reporter to editor in chief. I led the paper through cuts to our print schedule and grew our online audience while covering university and local news.

After I graduated, I interned for the Daily Press before I was hired as a breaking news reporter in July 2018. I covered a morning cops shift and other general assignment topics including higher education and transportation before I took over the Peninsula education beat in March 2020.

I currently cover K-12 and higher education in Newport News, Hampton and surrounding cities and counties in southeastern Virginia. Although I’ve written about everything from UFOs to a shipwrecked barge, my main areas of interest include state education policy and funding, teaching and learning in the pandemic, community colleges and equity and inclusion efforts.

I’m nonbinary and use they/them pronouns. Outside of work, I’m an avid hiker and pianist who’s trying to get better at cooking. I have the personality of a feral oppossum. I'm not going to explain that.
