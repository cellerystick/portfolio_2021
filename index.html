+ bio

I was a teenager when 61% of North Carolina voters voted for Amendment 1, defining marriage as between “one man and one woman” in the state constitution.

Growing up in Greensboro, I had considered the fact that I might be queer. The possibility that I might not be straight was terrifying. I heard the jokes from my classmates, watched the evening news, read the op-eds in the paper.

The lack of access, representation and compassion in journalism helped uphold the status quo then. It still does now. Done well, journalism is a catalyst for change and a force for good. But when journalists are disconnected from their communities and all of the people in them, it does a disservice to everyone.

I’ve been a journalist professionally since I graduated Virginia Tech with a degree in mathematics in 2018. I went on to intern for the Daily Press before being hired as a breaking news reporter in July 2018.

Currently, I’m a news planner for Gannett, helping newspapers in Illinois and Indiana coordinate and plan their coverage. I’ve covered a number of different beats and topics as a reporter, including education, state politics and crime for The Virginian-Pilot and Daily Press. Although I’ve written about a little bit of everything, some focuses include education policy and equity, particularly trans kids.

I’m trans myself — I use they/them and she/her pronouns to refer to myself. My passion is building better journalism, coverage that really listens to communities and responds to them, whatever that may look like.
